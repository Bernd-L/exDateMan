Usage

Using ExDateMan is easy. Just follow these steps:

  1. Log in or register
  2. Create an inventory or open an existing one.
  3. Pick a thing or create a new one

Definitions

ExDateMan uses specific words with closely defined meanings:

Inventory

The term Inventory refers to a distinct collection of things which is defined by its unique set of owners.
Each inventory has an owner. Other users may also be allowed to view or edit the inventory or its contents, depending on their role in a given inventory.

User

Someone who is registered in the ExDateMan service. Each user has:

  • A user-name (used for easy identification)
  • An email address (used for authentication and identification)
  • A passphrase (stored securely; used for authentication)

Furthermore, each user can have one relation per inventory, which is governed by the users role in said inventory.

Role

A role governs a users rights in any given inventory. A user may have one or zero roles per inventory.
If a user doesn't have a role in an inventory, the inventory doesn't get displayed to the user.

Thing

The term thing refers to something stored in an inventory, though not as a piece.

Stock

Every stock is an instance of a thing, and associated with one. One thing can have as many or as little (including 0) stocks as needed.
Each stock has:

  • Expiration date
  • Date opened
  • Use up in (days)
  • Quantity
  • Percentage left

Category

Every inventory can have categories. Every category can be the parent of other categories, allowing for the formation of sub- and super-category structures.
